📖 What is GreenComp?

GreenComp is the EU's sustainability competence framework. The Joint Research Centre (JRC) developed it in 2022. It gives learners and educators a shared language for sustainability – defining what it means as a competence you can learn and teach.

It works for all learners, all ages, all settings – formal, non-formal, informal. The goal: help people develop the knowledge, skills and attitudes to think, plan and act sustainably.

Why GreenComp?
  • Common language for sustainability across EU education
  • Foundation for training design and curriculum development
  • Measurable competence framework with KSA statements
  • Directly linked to EU Green Deal and Erasmus+ Green Priority
  • Referenced in the 2022 Council Recommendation on learning for sustainability
  • Supports both Union of Skills and Preparedness Union agendas
🐝 The Bee Metaphor

GreenComp uses bee pollination as a metaphor: Beehive = Values (protecting & sustaining), Pollen = Complexity (connecting systems), Flowers = Futures (generating new possibilities), Bees = Action (individual & collective agency). The four areas are tightly interrelated.

Structure

4 competence areas × 3 competences = 12 sustainability competences. All are equally important and interrelated.

🌍 GreenComp in the European Policy Framework
2019
EU Green Deal
Europe's roadmap to climate neutrality by 2050
▼

The European Green Deal is the EU's growth strategy for a modern, resource-efficient economy. Goal: Net-zero emissions by 2050.

→ Relevance for GreenComp:
Education and skills are key – GreenComp provides the competence framework for implementation.
🔗 View Document
2022
Erasmus+ Green Transition Strategy
Implementation Guidelines for the green priority
▼

Defines how Erasmus+ and ESC contribute to the green transition. Focus: Green Skills, sustainable mobility, environmentally friendly practices. GreenComp is explicitly referenced as the framework.

→ Relevance for GreenComp:
GreenComp is the reference framework for 'green skills'. NAs should use it for training and evaluation.
🔗 View Document
2022
Council Recommendation
Learning for environmental sustainability
▼

Calls on all Member States to integrate sustainability as cross-cutting theme in all education areas. GreenComp should serve as common reference.

→ Relevance for GreenComp:
Legal basis for integrating GreenComp into national education systems across the EU.
🔗 View Document
2025
Union of Skills
EU strategy to address skills shortages
▼

Green skills are key for the green transition and circular economy. Strategy calls for upskilling and reskilling across sectors, including those with lower and middle skills.

→ Relevance for GreenComp:
GreenComp provides the competence framework for the 'green skills' dimension. Directly connected to Erasmus+ programme priorities.
🔗 View Document
2025
Preparedness Union Strategy
Strengthening Europe's resilience against crises
▼

Launched March 2025 with 30 key actions. Aims to make Europe more resilient against climate disasters, pandemics, cyber threats and geopolitical tensions. Key for education: Preparedness shall be integrated into school curricula, an EU Preparedness Day introduced, and youth programmes shall foster preparedness skills.

→ Relevance for GreenComp:
Connects to GreenComp via 2.1 (Systems Thinking for crises), 3.2 (Adaptability) and 4.1 (Political Agency). The second policy pillar of Note 025 alongside Union of Skills.
🔗 View Document
2026
Note 025: TCA Thematic Focus
EU Commission aligns TCAs with Union of Skills & Preparedness Union
▼

From 2026, TCA/NET activities should align with both 'Union of Skills' AND 'Preparedness Union Strategy'. These are the two policy pillars of Note 025. NAs should map existing activities to both frameworks, identify synergies, and develop new aligned activities. Existing LTA sustainability work including this GreenComp toolkit is a good match.

→ Relevance for GreenComp:
GreenComp directly supports both pillars: green skills (Union of Skills) and resilience competences (Preparedness). This toolkit helps NAs demonstrate alignment.
